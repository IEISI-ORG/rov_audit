remarks:        -------------------------- BGP Routing Policy ------------------------------
remarks:        DTEL-IX Route Servers support following RFC and Drafts:
remarks:        * RFC 1997 BGP Communities Attribute;
remarks:        * RFC 4360 BGP Extended Communities Attribute;
remarks:        * RFC 4384 BGP Communities for Data Collection;
remarks:        * RFC 4893, 6793 BGP Support for Four-Octet Autonomous System (AS) Number Space;
remarks:        * RFC 7947 Internet Exchange BGP Route Server;
remarks:        * RFC 7999 BLACKHOLE Community
remarks:        * RFC 8092 BGP Large Community Attribute;
remarks:        * draft-hilliard-ix-bgp-route-server-operations-03;
remarks:        Route-servers DO NOT modify, process or remove communities received from customers except
remarks:        those which are processed by the route-servers themselves:
remarks:        +-------------+--------------------+-------------------+---------------------------------------+
remarks:        | RFC1997 | RFC4360 | RFC8092 | Meaning |
remarks:        +-------------+--------------------+-------------------+---------------------------------------+
remarks:        | 0:31210 | target:0:31210 | 31210:0:31210 | Do not announce route to any peer |
remarks:        | 0:X | target:0:X | 31210:0:X | Do not announce route to AS X |
remarks:        | 31210:X | target:31210:X | 31210:31210:X | Announce route only to AS X |
remarks:        | 31210:31210 | target:31210:31210 | 31210:31210:31210 | Announce route to all peers (default)|
remarks:        +-------------+--------------------+-------------------+---------------------------------------+
remarks:        CAVEATS:
remarks:        * RFC1997 community attributes are applicable only to selective announces towards 16-bit ASNs.
remarks:        For announcements towards 32-bit ASNs please use Extended or Large community attributes.
remarks:        * RFC1997 communities 'no-export' and 'no-advertise' are also supported. Other RFC1997
remarks:        well-known communities are not supported and stripped.
remarks:        * Global blocking community 0:31210 has the highest precedence. For example, if a route have
remarks:        communities 0:31210 and 31210:X then it will not be announced to any peer, including AS X.
remarks:        * JunOS does not support BGP Extended Community target:0:X. On Juniper routers please use
remarks:        BGP Large Community attributes.
remarks:        Prepending your own AS number is supported using BGP Large Community Attribute:
remarks:        +-------------------+-------------------------------------------------------------------------+
remarks:        | RFC8092 | Meaning |
remarks:        +-------------------+-------------------------------------------------------------------------+
remarks:        | 31210:6500X:Y | Prepend your own AS X times when announcing route to AS Y |
remarks:        | 31210:6500X:31210 | Prepend your own AS X times when announcing route to all peers |
remarks:        +-------------------+-------------------------------------------------------------------------+
remarks:        CAVEATS:
remarks:        * 1 <= X <= 3
remarks:        ---- Simple Remote Triggered Blackhole --------------------------------------------------------
remarks:        Simple RTBH allows customer to discard ALL traffic towards certain host is its network on
remarks:        DTEL-IX edge. Only /32 (IPv4) and longer or equal than /56 (IPv6) routes are accepted.
remarks:        To blackhole certain prefix please use simultaneously communities BLACKHOLE* and 31210:X
remarks:        (or target:31210:X) where X is an AS number of peer from which traffic should be blackholed.
remarks:        If X is 31210 then traffic will be blackholed from all peers.
remarks:        * According to RFC7999, BLACKHOLE community is 65535:666.
remarks:        ---- Advanced Remote Triggered Blackhole ------------------------------------------------------
remarks:        Advanced RTBH allows customer to redirect traffic to DTEL-IX firewall and instruct it what one
remarks:        of predefined traffic types should be discarded. Communities used for that are the same as for
remarks:        Simple RTBH but with additional extended community which defines traffic type:
remarks:        +---------------------------+-------------------------+-------------------------+
remarks:        | Rule | Drop Community* | Shape Community** |
remarks:        +---------------------------+-------------------------+-------------------------+
remarks:        | All UDP Traffic | target:31210:1017000000 | target:31210:1117000000 |
remarks:        | UDP, Src Port 53 (DNS) | target:31210:1017000005 | target:31210:1117000005 |
remarks:        | UDP, Src Port 123 (NTP) | target:31210:1017000010 | target:31210:1117000010 |
remarks:        | UDP, Src Port 389 (LDAP) | target:31210:1017000050 | target:31210:1117000050 |
remarks:        | UDP, Src Port 1900 (SSDP) | target:31210:1017000100 | target:31210:1117000100 |
remarks:        +---------------------------+-------------------------+-------------------------+
remarks:        * Drop Community = Drop all packets matching the rule
remarks:        ** Shape Community = Shape all packets matching the rule to maximum 5 Mbit/s
